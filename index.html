Freight surge, port delays hit rice exports; 4 lakh tonne of basmati at risk: Exporters Federation

Indian rice exports are facing disruption as rising marine fuel costs, higher freight rates and shipping delays impact shipments, according to Dev Garg of the Indian Rice Exporters Federation. Around 3,000 containers of basmati rice are stuck at ports such as Mundra and Kandla, while large quantities remain in transit or at destination ports. With freight costs rising sharply and insurance premiums increasing, exporters warn that nearly 3.5–4 lakh tonne of basmati rice shipments are currently at risk.
